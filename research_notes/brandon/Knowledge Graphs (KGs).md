# Overview
- Entities and concepts are represented as nodes
- Relationships between nodes are depicted as edges
	- KGs can capture semantic meanings of nodes
- Examples: [Google's Knowledge Graph](https://support.google.com/knowledgepanel/answer/9787176?hl=en)
- <mark>They are static, and capture knowledge up to a certain point in time</mark>
	- Online search KGs have to be updated as new entities and knowledge emerge
	- KGs that capture user data have to update dynamically as new users are created and follow other accounts

# Related technologies
- Knowledge graphs that incorporate temporal information have been developed, including:
	- [[Dynamic Knowledge Graphs (DKGs)]]
	- [[Temporal Knowledge Graphs (TKGs)]]
