# Overview
MIMIC-III is a large, freely-available database comprising deidentified health-related data associated with <mark>over forty thousand patients who stayed in critical care units of the Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center between 2001 and 2012</mark>. The database includes information such as demographics, vital sign measurements made at the bedside (~1 data point per hour), laboratory test results, procedures, medications, caregiver notes, imaging reports, and mortality (including post-hospital discharge).

MIMIC supports a diverse range of analytic studies spanning epidemiology, clinical decision-rule improvement, and electronic tool development. It is notable for three factors: <mark>it is freely available to researchers worldwide; it encompasses a diverse and very large population of ICU patients; and it contains highly granular data, including vital signs, laboratory results, and medications.</mark>
